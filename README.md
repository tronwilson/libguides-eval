# Overview
As LibGuides is the primary means by which we provide information about us to our patrons and stakeholders it is vital that we ensure that the content up-to-date, relevant, useful and accessible. In addition, as our digital landscape is becoming increasingly saturated with synthetic content, the information provided through LibGuides can take on an increasingly significant role of being a source of digital provenance for legal research information.  
## Scope
To this end, I propose that we re-evaluate our LibGuides along the following three axes: 
1. *Audience & Purpose* -  the particular group at whom the content is aimed or for whom it is intended, e.g. law professors, law students, general public, etc. 
2. _Look & Feel_ - the overall presentation of content on the site from typographic choices to layout. 
3. _Functionality & Accessibility_ - the balance between presenting content in a appealing and functional way while ensuring that content is accessible to the widest possible audience.   
### Audience & Purpose
Generally we should structure our content based on the audience and purpose of the guide. So for example, if we create a general information guide that should probably have a different template and structure than a guide that's created as a list of resources for a specific class.
### Presentation of Content
Once we have defined our audience and our guides have a clear purpose what is the best way to display that content so that it's maximally useable? Are some font choice better than others? What font size should be use for various kinds of content? What colors should we use to signify different content categories? etc, etc. 
### Functionality & Accessibility
Finally, we want to balance any aesthetic decisions against the ability of (a) users to equitably access the content and (b) the content creators ability to maintain the content. Ultimately we want to make sure that the site looks good, provides relevant and useful content, but at the same time is easy to maintain and is accessible to the widest possible audience. 

## Conclusion 
Overall, I'd like for us to be able to develop some clear demonstrations and have some concrete recommendations that we can take to the Library decision makers in order to enact some policy changes for the creation and maintenance of LibGudes which should result in a better development experience for the content creators and an improved user experience for our intended audience.
